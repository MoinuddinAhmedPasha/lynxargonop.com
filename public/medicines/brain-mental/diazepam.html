Diazepam

A benzodiazepine used for anxiety relief, muscle relaxation, seizures, and sleep disorders.

What is Diazepam?

Diazepam is a benzodiazepine medication that acts on the central nervous system to produce calming, sedative, muscle-relaxant, and anticonvulsant effects.

History & Background

Diazepam was introduced in the 1960s and quickly became one of the most widely prescribed psychoactive drugs due to its effectiveness and versatility.

Chemical Structure & Properties

  • IUPAC name: 7-chloro-1-methyl-5-phenyl-1,3-dihydro-2H-1,4-benzodiazepin-2-one
  • Molecular formula: C₁₆H₁₃ClN₂O
  • Molar mass: 284.74 g/mol
  • Functional groups: Benzodiazepine ring, ketone, aromatic rings

Mechanism of Action

Diazepam enhances the effect of GABA by:

  • Increasing GABA-A receptor activity
  • Increasing chloride ion influx
  • Reducing neuronal excitability

Pharmacokinetics

  • Absorption: Rapid oral absorption
  • Peak plasma time: 1–2 hours
  • Half-life: 20–50 hours
  • Metabolism: Liver (active metabolites)
  • Excretion: Urine

Medical Uses

  • Anxiety disorders
  • Insomnia (short-term)
  • Muscle spasms
  • Status epilepticus
  • Alcohol withdrawal

Dosage Guidelines

Adults: 2–10 mg, 2–4 times daily (varies by indication)

⚠️ Use lowest effective dose for shortest duration.

Side Effects

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Muscle weakness
  • Memory impairment

Warnings & Precautions

  • Risk of dependence and tolerance
  • Avoid alcohol
  • Do not stop abruptly

Drug Interactions

  • Alcohol
  • Opioids
  • Other sedatives

Frequently Asked Questions

Is diazepam addictive? – Yes, if misused.

Does it help sleep? – Short-term only.

Is it a muscle relaxant? – Yes.

Is it safe long-term? – Not recommended.
