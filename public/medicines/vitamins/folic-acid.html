Folic Acid (Vitamin B9)

A vital B-complex vitamin essential for DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, and fetal development.

What is Folic Acid?

Folic acid is the synthetic form of Vitamin B9, a water-soluble vitamin required for cell division and genetic material synthesis.

It is especially important during pregnancy.

History & Background

Folic acid was identified while studying nutritional causes of anemia and later found crucial in preventing neural tube defects.

Chemical Structure & Properties

  • IUPAC name: (2S)-2-[(4-{[(2-amino-4-oxo-1,4-dihydropteridin-6-yl)methyl]amino}benzoyl)amino]pentanedioic acid
  • Molecular formula: C₁₉H₁₉N₇O₆
  • Molar mass: 441.40 g/mol
  • Nature: Pteridine derivative

Mechanism of Action

Folic acid functions by:

  • Participating in DNA and RNA synthesis
  • Supporting red blood cell formation
  • Reducing homocysteine levels

Health Benefits & Uses

  • Prevention of neural tube defects
  • Megaloblastic anemia
  • Support during pregnancy
  • Cardiovascular health support

Dosage Guidelines

Adults: 400 mcg/day

Pregnancy: 400–600 mcg/day

⚠️ Excess intake may mask vitamin B12 deficiency.

Vitamin B9 Deficiency

  • Megaloblastic anemia
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Mouth ulcers
  • Birth defects in fetus

Side Effects

  • Nausea (high doses)
  • Bloating
  • Sleep disturbances (rare)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is folic acid same as folate? – Synthetic form.

Is it important in pregnancy? – Yes.

Is it water-soluble? – Yes.

Can men take folic acid? – Yes.
